How the 3×8 matrix works in TonForce

A simple and clear explanation of the matrix structure mechanics and the 4-level deep referral program.

💎

The matrix structure is the foundation of TonForce. How well you understand its mechanics determines your income. In this article, we break down the mechanics in simple terms.

What is a 3×8 matrix

A matrix is a structure where each participant can have 3 direct referrals (that's the "3" in the name), and the matrix itself consists of 8 levels (that's the "8"). Each subsequent level is 3 times more expensive than the previous one.

Level pricing

  • Level 1 — 5 TON (Starter)
  • Level 2 — 15 TON (Bronze)
  • Level 3 — 45 TON (Silver)
  • Level 4 — 135 TON (Gold)
  • Level 5 — 405 TON (Platinum)
  • Level 6 — 1,215 TON (Diamond)
  • Level 7 — 3,645 TON (VIP)
  • Level 8 — 10,935 TON (Black)

Referral program: R1, R2, R3, R4

This is the most interesting part. When someone buys a level, the payout doesn't go directly to you (like in a simple affiliate program), but to a specific depth of the matrix. The depth depends on the level.

Distribution logic

  • Levels 1 and 5 → payout goes to your direct referrer (R1 — the one who invited you)
  • Levels 2 and 6 → payout goes to your referrer's referrer (R2)
  • Levels 3 and 7 → payout goes to depth level 3 (R3)
  • Levels 4 and 8 → payout goes to depth level 4 (R4)

What happens if the upline is inactive?

If the upline at the required level doesn't have the corresponding level activated, the payout is directed to the project wallet. This motivates participants to keep their levels active.

Overflow — why it matters

When someone "above" you in the matrix gets a 4th referral (exceeding the limit of 3), the new participant "drops down" — potentially under you. This is called overflow, and it means you can receive payouts from people you didn't personally invite.

The higher you are in the matrix and the more people below you, the more often overflow works in your favor.
